Barry Manilow's Net Worth Allowed Him And His Secret Spouse To Enjoy An Absolutely Lavish Lifestyle

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, July 24, 2024

Highlights

  • Barry Manilow's net worth is a staggering $100 million, due to his successful music career.
  • Manilow found love with his husband Garry Kief in 1978, but hid their relationship for years.
  • Kief not only manages Manilow's career but is also his partner in life.

Music legend Barry Manilow has had a consistent presence in the industry since his heyday in the 1970s. Known for his showmanship and elaborate performances, Manilow remains relevant today through his touring and residencies.

Manilow came out as gay when he was 73 years old, holding onto an important personal secret for four decades, according to People Magazine. Since 1978, Manilow has been in a relationship with his now spouse, Garry Kief. Due to Manilow's net worth, he and Kief have lived extraordinary lives. In this article, we will discuss Manilow's net worth, his relationship with Kief, and the lavish lifestyle they live.

What Is Barry Manilow's Net Worth And What Is His Lifestyle Like?

Considering Barry Manilow's illustrious career, it is far from surprising that the iconic performer enjoys a wealthy lifestyle. At the height of his fame, Manilow released hit single after hit single and rejuvenated the career of legendary music executive, Clive Davis. Davis, who is recognized as a monumental force in the business, had some creative differences with Kelly Clarkson.

Manilow has owned or rented a few noteworthy properties throughout his career. In 2016, a Malibu estate that Manilow once owned went on the market for $16.75 million! Singer Pink eventually purchased Manilow's former home in June 2021, but put it back on the market that October, according to the New York Post.

Manilow, known for his Las Vegas residencies, once rented a spacious condo in the Park Towers. In 2015, the condo was eventually brought to market for $2.2 million, according to the Las Vegas Review-Journal.

Who Is Barry Manilow's Spouse, Garry Kief, And When Did They Get Married?

Barry Manilow was once married to a woman named Susan Deixler from 1964 to 1966, according to Bang Showbiz NZ. High school sweethearts, Manilow found Deixler to be "the perfect wife", according to Heavy. In a People Magazine interview, Manilow admitted that he truly was attracted to his wife at the time, and that their annulment was due to Manilow's career goals rather than his romantic preferences.

"I was in love with Susan. I just was not ready for marriage...I was too young. I wasn't ready to settle down."

Once Manilow and Deixler broke up, Manilow began to explore his romantic preferences more and recognized that he may be gay. In 1978, Manilow met Garry Kief. According to People, Manilow was attracted to Kief the minute he saw him. "I knew that this was it. I was one of the lucky ones."

Manilow and Kief began to be a couple, but Manilow hid their relationship for years. He did not even share that they wed in April 2014 until years later. According to Manilow, "I thought I would be disappointing [my fans] if they knew I was gay. So, I never did anything. When they found out that Garry and I were together, they were so happy. The reaction was so beautiful...I'm just so grateful for it."

Manilow, on why he waited to share his marriage to Kief, articulated that,

"I'm so private. I always have been."

While Manilow's marriage to Kief became public knowledge in 2015, the legendary performer did not officially share that he was gay until 2017, according to People. Kief is not only Manilow's husband, but has been his manager for decades.

Suzanne Somers, of Three's Company fame, was a dear friend of Manilow's and Kief's until her passing. According to the actress, Kief was vital to Manilow's sanity and ultimate longevity. "There's Barry Manilow the performer, and then there's the Barry 'machine.' It takes enormous savvy and know-how to book and market complicated arena tours, choreograph promotion, direct the entire team and make it look effortless, and that part is Garry's domain. A major career takes two. Between them, there is enormous comfort and trust."
