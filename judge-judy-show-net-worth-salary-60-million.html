Did Judge Judy Really Make $60 Million On Her Hit Show?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, July 6, 2024

The small screen is a unique place that has given rise to a number of public figures hosting a variety of unique shows. Jerry Springer and Maury Povich, for instance, are two names that virtually everyone is familiar with thanks to the infamous shows that they have been hosting on television for years.

Judge Judy is one of the biggest names in television history, and she has the net worth to prove it. She has had an iconic career and has achieved greatness over time, and this was instrumental in her making a fortune.

So, how much was Judge Judy making while starring on her iconic show? Let's take a closer look at her monster salary and see how she made her millions.

Judge Judy Is An Iconic Televison Star

Unless a person has been living under a rock for the last 25 years, then they are no doubt aware of who Judge Judy is. Daytime television might not be for everyone, but many hosts have found huge audiences through the years. Judge Judy just so happens to be one of the most famous of all time.

The acclaimed figure spent years cutting her teeth as a lawyer before getting the chance to be a criminal court judge. She would steadily work her way up the ladder while also earning a reputation for the way that she handled things. This, in turn, played a huge role in her eventually making her way onto television.

After writing a book and having a 60 Minutes piece about her hit the small screen, Judge Judy was primed and ready to make her television debut.

Her Show Was A Huge Success

In September of 1996, Judge Judy made its debut on daytime television, and the reality court show was able to find a massive audience that tuned in loyaly to see the iconic host tackle cases. The premise was nothing new, but Judge Judy herself was such a compelling figure that people kept coming back for more during its 25-year run.

At this stage of her career, Judge Judy has nothing left to accomplish, and she's made enough money for several lifetimes. Despite this, she shows no signs of slowing down, and she has plans to keep things going.

"I’m not tired. I don’t play golf or tennis. I have no desire to learn how to play mahjong, chess or checkers. I know what I like to do. Why, at my stage in life, would I try to find something else when I already know what I like? And this isn’t a 9-to-5 job. I’ve still got the time to see the children I love, the grandchildren who are growing up very fast and the cute mate who I still get a kick out of," she told The Hollywood Reporter.

Judge Judy has done a number of impressive things during her time in Hollywood, and negotiating her sky-high salary just might be her most impressive feat.

She Made Millions

So, how much was Judge Judy making while starring in her iconic show? Details over time have emerged, and at her peak, Judge Judy was bringing home a staggering $60 million during her biggest years on television.

"I told the company that I worked for this: ‘I want to be more of a partner. Don’t treat me as a paid employee. I could make this show without you – I created a deal where I could do that – but you can’t make it without me. I can take Judy Sheindlin anywhere else. And good luck with you if you can find somebody else. Otherwise, let’s share the gift that this program has brought to both of us,’” said Judge Judy.

The iconic Judge Judy show has come to and end, but the star has moved onto a new show, Judy Justice, on Amazon.

When speaking on her new show, she said, "We have to deliver a certain number of episodes by December, and then Amazon will make the determination how and when they want to release this show. I don't know. Give me a robe and a case, and I'll do my job. I had wonderful people producing and directing the Judge Judy program, and a couple of them will be following me to Amazon. That will keep my life on a steady keel."

Judge Judy has long been one of the richest television stars in the world, and it's incredible to see the amount of work that she put in to hit her $60 million salary.
