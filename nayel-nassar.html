Nayel Nassar Wiki/Biography

The showjumper was brought into existence on the 21st of January in 1991 in Chicago, Illinois, USA. He was brought up in a business based Egyptian family in Kuwait. He completed his early education from the American School of Kuwait and after that he enrolled himself at Stanford University from where he completed his graduate course in Economics and Management.

Since childhood, he was fascinated by horse riding and wanted to pursue his career as a jockey, for his dream he also convinced his parents for allowing him to pursue his dream in this sport.

Siblings, Family, and Friends

The showjumper belongs to a Muslim Egyptian family in Chicago. He follows the Islamic religion and holds an American and Egyptian nationality. He also has one sibling, his elder brother who takes care of his family business.

Nayel Nassar’s marital status is engaged. He is currently engaged to his long term girlfriend Jennifer Gates. Jennifer Gates is none other than the daughter of Bill Gates who is the founder of Microsoft Company and among the richest people in the world. Career

The sportsman had a keen interest in horse riding since childhood. He is riding horses since he was 5 years old. His parents gifted him a horse at the age of 10 and supported his dream. He is a well-trained equestrian who got his professional training in Europe.

Nayel started his professional career in 2013 and since then he has participated in many events and won nearly 36 of them. He had played in FEI World Cup finals three times in 2013, 2014, and 2017. He also played the FEI World Equestrian Games in 2014. Recently he participated at the Longines FEI World Cup finals in Paris and he regularly competes on the international Grand Prix Circuit.

The sportsman also played an important role in securing Egypt’s spot at the Olympics Games to be held in Tokyo in 2020. This is the first time in the last 60 years that Egypt has its equestrian team at Olympics games. They reached there after winning the CSI04 National Cup competition which was held at Rabat in Morocco in October 2019 in which Nayel played an important role in securing the win for his country and team.

Along with his professional career in sports, he is also a well-known businessman who owned a company named Nassar Stables LLC in 2014 in Encinitas, San Diego.
